About Me

I'm Nicolas Viulet, a neuroscience researcher currently pursuing my Master of Science at McMaster University, where I'm investigating the relationship between intracortical myelin and major depressive disorder. I'm passionate about understanding how the brain works and how we can leverage neuroimaging data to improve mental health outcomes, develop new diagnostic protocols, and improve therapeutic approaches for neurological disorders. I hope to develop my computational and clinical skills in neuroscience by working at the intersection of scientific research and clinical applications.

Beyond my current research, my background in Buddhism, Psychology, and Mental Health has shaped how I think about neuroscience—not just as a technical discipline, but as a way to understand the complexity human experience. I believe in the importance of interdisciplinary collaboration and integrating mindfulness into every aspect of my academic and personal life.

When I'm not analyzing brain imaging data or reading research papers, you'll probably find me experimenting in the kitchen or perfecting my coffee brewing technique. There's something deeply satisfying about the precision and creativity that goes into both cooking and making a great cup of coffee—not unlike designing a good experiment! I'm currently looking for opportunities to contribute to innovative neuroscience research and continue growing as both a scientist and a mentor.
